Assorted Sashimi from Gallery Court Ofa Atu

In May 2015, my husband and I stayed at a very unique hotel in Kawazu, Japan. The hotel, "Gallery Court Ofa Atu," is a family business owned by the Kashiwamura family. It is not just a hotel, but also a gallery that displays many paintings by the owner's parents. We didn't expect the dinner to be very good because it is located in the middle of nowhere. However, the dinner was surprisingly delicious. Everything was home-made, and the freshly baked bread was delicious. My favoriate is the assorted sashimi (raw fish) that includes lobster and abalone. My husband is really picky about food, but even he said this chef is really good and he could be very successful if he opens up a restaurant in Tokyo.

Okonomiyaki from Yukari

Yukari is conveniently located in the Yokohama Sky Building right outside of the Yokohama Station. They sell okonomiyaki, chow mein (stir-fried noodles), and more. Okonomiyaki is basically a pancake with variety of toppings, such as meat, seafood, cabbage, green onion, etc. You can also have okonomiyaki with chow mein, so the best option for two is to order one regular okonomiyaki, and one with chow mein. You can grill your own okonomiyaki, but you can also ask the waitress to help as well. Honestly speaking, okonomiyaki is easy to make and the okonomiyaki at this restaurant wasn't amazing.

Matcha Soba from Tsukiji Sarashinanosato

You might heard of Tsukiji market, but you might not associate it with soba. Tsukiji Sarashinanosato is a restaurant that specializes in soba, or buckwheat noodle. Soba noodles are served either in hot broth as a noodle soup, or chilled with a dipping sauce. Even though it is next to the fish market, it was still packed when we came. The soba here were chewy with grainy texture, and the dipping sauce is a bit on the salty side. Therefore, I suggest just dip the noodles lightly, so the delicate taste of soba isn't overwhelmed by the dipping sauce.

Curry from Oxymoron

Have you ever had "dry" curry? Oxymoron in Kamakura serves curry, a name that you cannot associate the food with. Their curry is sauceless with lots of herbs such as cilantro, green onion, and parsley. The taste of their "curry" is very similar to fried rice, and I thought the ground meat on top of the rice balances perfectly with the herbs. I'd say it's a must-try if you are going to Kamakura, but just be aware that the line may be line since the restaurant is quite popular.
